Cyberbullying significantly impacts mental health, often leading to depression, anxiety, and even suicidal thoughts, especially among adolescents. Victims may experience low self-esteem, feelings of isolation, and changes in sleep and eating patterns. The unique features of online interactions, like the potential for anonymity and the speed of information sharing, can exacerbate these negative effects.

What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital technology to harass, threaten, or humiliate someone. It can happen on social media, messaging apps, gaming platforms, or any online space.

Effects of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying can lead to anxiety, depression, and in severe cases, suicidal thoughts. It's a serious issue that affects children, teens, and even adults across the globe.
